Silly!

BREAKING: Local Man Turns X.com into Sketchbook!

In a shocking turn of events, users are reporting that their social media feeds are beginning to look like hand-drawn newspapers from the 1920s. The phenomenon, caused by the "X Silly Feed" extension, uses experimental CSS to replace rigid boxes with organic, wiggly shapes.

Fig 1. A very serious representation of a silly feed.

"I couldn't take the doomscrolling anymore," says creator Túlio Sousa. "Now, every terrible take looks like it was scribbled by a frantic Victorian journalist. It's much better."

Organic Borders

Uses the cutting-edge `border-shape` property to create hand-drawn effects without heavy images or complex SVG filters. Pure CSS magic.

Subtle paper grains and ink-like typography transport your feed from Silicon Valley to a dusty newsstand.
